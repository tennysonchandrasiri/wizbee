The Princess and the Pea

Story

One stormy night, lightning lit up the royal castle. A knock sounded at the gate.

King: “Who could be out in this weather?”

The guard opened the door to a young woman, soaked from head to toe.

Young Woman: “Please, may I stay the night? I’m a princess.”

Queen (whispering to the King): “A princess? We must be sure. I have a test.”

The Queen went to the guest room, placed a tiny green pea on the bedframe, and piled twenty mattresses and twenty featherbeds on top.

Queen (smiling): “There. If she’s a real princess, she’ll feel it.”

The next morning, the Queen asked:

Queen: “How did you sleep, dear?”

Princess: “Oh, terribly! Something hard was under the bed. My body aches all over.”

Queen (clapping her hands): “Only a true princess could feel a pea through so many layers!”

King (laughing): “Then she shall marry our son.”

The Prince and the Princess smiled at each other, and the tiny pea was placed in the royal treasure room, a reminder that true nobility shows in the smallest details.

Moral: Real sensitivity cannot be hidden.
