The Wolf in Sheep’s Clothing

Story

One sunny morning, a wolf spotted a flock of sheep.

Wolf (thinking): “Hmm… I’m hungry, but the shepherd is watching. Wait… what’s that? A sheepskin!”

Wolf: “Perfect! If I wear this, I can walk right among them.”

The wolf draped the skin over himself and strolled into the field.

Sheep 1: “Hello, new friend! Where did you come from?”

Wolf (in a gentle voice): “Oh, I… wandered away from another flock.”

Sheep 2: “You look tired. Come graze with us.”

Wolf (grinning inside): “Thank you, I will.”

All day, the wolf pretended to eat grass but dreamed of lamb chops.

Wolf (thinking): “Tonight, I’ll choose the fattest one.”

Evening came. The shepherd entered the pen.

Shepherd: “Ah, there’s my supper for tonight. This one looks plump.”

He grabbed the “sheep” wearing the skin — the wolf!

Wolf: “Wait! I’m not—”

Too late. The shepherd ended the wolf’s trick forever.

Moral: Deceiving others often leads to your own downfall.
